# AI Monitoring

After you deploy [!UICONTROL AI Assistant], you can use [!UICONTROL Monitoring] to get visibility into how it is used and performs. It lets you understand who uses [!UICONTROL AI Assistant], which tasks it supports, where it succeeds or struggles, and how that ties to adoption, cost, and business impact.

AI Monitoring is organized around three areas: 

* **Adoption and engagement** in the [!UICONTROL Usage] dashboard
* **Quality and performance** in [!UICONTROL Conversation Replay] and feedback
* **Cost and consumption** in the Credits dashboard

Together they help you operate [!UICONTROL AI Assistant] with clear data instead of guesswork.

## Usage dashboard

The Usage dashboard is the central place for adoption and engagement across your organization. It connects high-level trends to deeper analysis. From any metric in the dashboard, you can drill into individual conversations to see what drives the numbers.

**What you can review**

* **Prompts over time**: Overall usage growth and adoption trends.
* **Active users and conversations**: How broadly the assistant is adopted across users.
* **Average prompts per conversation**: Depth of engagement and whether interactions are substantive.
* **Feedback (thumbs up / down)**: Early signals on quality and user satisfaction.

## Conversation Replay with feedback

[!UICONTROL Conversation Replay] shows actual AI interactions, not only aggregates. You reach it from the Usage dashboard when you need to move from trends to specific exchanges.

**What you can review**

* **Prompt and response history**: The user's question and [!UICONTROL AI Assistant] replies, as delivered.
* **Feedback signals**: Which interactions users marked positively or negatively, to prioritize improvements.
* **Patterns over time**: Recurring themes in strong and weak experiences across many conversations.

This view helps you go beyond roll-ups to see what works, where users hit friction, and how to improve response quality over time. Usage trends answer _what_ is happening. Conversation replay helps explain _why_.

## [!UICONTROL Credits] dashboard

Once you understand usage and quality, the Credits dashboard answers how that activity translates into consumption and cost.

**What you can review**

* **Total credits consumed**: Overall AI Assistant usage in credit terms.
* **Daily and monthly trends**: Spikes, dips, and changes in usage patterns.
* **Credits remaining**: What is left in your allocation so you can plan before limits or overages.

Use this view to forecast usage, align with budget, and allocate credits across teams more predictably.

## User Feedback dashboard

The User Feedback dashboard helps organizations review detailed user feedback submitted for AI Assistant interactions. It provides visibility into which conversations users marked positively or negatively and allows administrators and Centers of Excellence (COEs) to investigate the interaction details behind the feedback.
From feedback summaries, users can drill into individual conversations to review prompts, responses, reasoning details, and feedback notes associated with the interaction.

What you can review

**Feedback trends over time:** Understand how user feedback changes across workflows and time periods.
**Thumbs up and thumbs down feedback:** Review positive and negative interaction signals submitted by users.
**Feedback categories:** Understand the types of responses users identified as helpful or problematic.
**Conversation-level drill-downs:** Move from feedback summaries into the individual conversations tied to feedback events.
**Prompt and response history:** Review the user prompts and AI Assistant responses associated with submitted feedback.
**Feedback details and notes:** Review additional context and comments provided by users during feedback submission.

This dashboard helps organizations better understand user experiences, investigate issues, and identify opportunities to improve AI Assistant quality and effectiveness over time.

## Active Users dashboard

The Active Users dashboard helps organizations understand how AI Assistant adoption and engagement vary across users over time. It provides visibility into who is actively using AI Assistant, how frequently they engage, and which workflows drive the most activity across the organization.
From user-level summaries, administrators and Centers of Excellence (COEs) can drill into individual user activity and conversations to better understand engagement patterns and usage behavior.

What you can review

* **Active users over time:** Track adoption and engagement trends across the organization.
* **User engagement patterns:** Understand how frequently users interact with AI Assistant and how engagement changes over time.
* **Conversation activity:** Review the number of conversations and prompts generated by individual users.
* **Top active users:** Identify highly engaged users and teams driving AI Assistant adoption.
* **User-level drill-downs:** Move from aggregate engagement metrics into individual user activity and conversation details.
* **Usage trends by workflow:** Understand which workflows and use cases generate the most engagement across users.
* **Engagement consistency:** Identify users with recurring engagement patterns as well as users with declining activity over time.

This dashboard helps organizations measure adoption, identify engagement trends, and better understand how AI Assistant is being used across teams and workflows.
